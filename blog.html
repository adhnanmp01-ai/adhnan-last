Agricultural Exports

How to Import Rice from India: A Complete Buyer's Guide

Infinomex Global Private Limited · Export Guides

India is the world's largest exporter of rice, supplying both basmati and non-basmati varieties to buyers across the Middle East, Africa, and Southeast Asia. For first-time importers, understanding the process can feel overwhelming — this guide breaks down what you actually need to know.

1. Choosing the Right Variety

Basmati rice (long-grain, aromatic) is typically preferred in Middle Eastern and Gulf markets, while non-basmati varieties such as Sona Masuri, IR64, and parboiled rice are popular across Africa due to their value pricing and cooking properties suited to local cuisines.

2. Certifications That Matter

  • APEDA registration — mandatory for agricultural exports from India, confirms the exporter is authorized by the Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority.
  • FSSAI license — confirms food safety compliance under Indian law.
  • Phytosanitary certificate — issued per shipment, required by most importing countries' customs authorities.

3. Typical Shipping Terms

Most Indian rice exports move on FOB (Free on Board) or CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) terms from major ports including Kandla, Mundra, Chennai, and Kochi. Container loads (FCL) are standard for orders above 20-25 tonnes; smaller buyers can often arrange LCL or consolidated shipments through their supplier.

4. Working with a Verified Exporter

Always verify an exporter's IEC (Importer Exporter Code), GST registration, and APEDA/FSSAI numbers before placing an order. Reputable exporters will share these openly and provide sample reports, lab test certificates, and references on request.

Spice Exports

APEDA & FSSAI Certified Spice Exporters: What International Buyers Should Know

Infinomex Global Private Limited · Export Guides

India has been the world's spice capital for centuries, and Kerala in particular remains a hub for high-quality pepper, cardamom, ginger, turmeric, and other spices. But for an international buyer, "Indian spices" covers an enormous range of quality levels — here's how to identify a reliable supplier.

1. Why Certifications Are Non-Negotiable

Spice shipments are subject to strict pesticide-residue and microbial testing in most importing countries, particularly the EU and Gulf states. An FSSAI license confirms the exporter operates under India's food safety regulatory framework, while APEDA registration is required for export documentation and customs clearance.

2. Quality Grades and Specifications

Before ordering, buyers should request specifications such as moisture content, volatile oil percentage (for pepper and cardamom), curcumin content (for turmeric), and ASTA cleanliness ratings. A trustworthy exporter will provide a Certificate of Analysis (COA) for each shipment.

3. Packaging & Shelf Life

Spices for export are typically packed in food-grade PP bags, vacuum-sealed pouches, or jute bags lined with polyethylene, depending on the destination climate and shipping duration. Proper packaging is critical to prevent moisture absorption and aroma loss during long sea voyages.

4. Common Destination Markets

Kerala-origin spices are in strong demand across the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and broader GCC, as well as growing markets in East and West Africa and parts of Europe.

Steel Scrap & Metals

Importing Steel Scrap from India: Process, Documentation & Working with Verified Exporters

Infinomex Global Private Limited · Export Guides

India's growing industrial base generates significant volumes of recyclable steel scrap, making it an increasingly important sourcing destination for steel mills and recyclers in the Middle East, Africa, and Southeast Asia. Here's what buyers need to know before placing an order.

1. Types of Steel Scrap Commonly Exported

  • HMS (Heavy Melting Scrap) 1 & 2 — the most commonly traded grade
  • Shredded scrap — processed for higher density and easier melting
  • Industrial machinery scrap and structural offcuts

2. Required Documentation

Exporters must hold a valid IEC (Importer Exporter Code) issued by DGFT and be GST registered. For each shipment, buyers should expect a commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, and (where applicable) a pre-shipment inspection report from an internationally recognized inspection agency.

3. Inspection & Quality Assurance

Given the variability in scrap quality, many buyers arrange third-party inspection (e.g., SGS, Bureau Veritas, or equivalent) before loading. Reputable exporters will accommodate inspection requests and provide photos/videos of the cargo prior to shipment.

4. Shipping & Logistics

Steel scrap is typically shipped via bulk vessels or in open-top containers, depending on quantity. Major Indian ports handling scrap exports include Mundra, Kandla, and Chennai.
